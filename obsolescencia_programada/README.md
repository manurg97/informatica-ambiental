# Programmed Obsolescence

Programmed obsolescence refers to the practice of intentionally designing products with a limited lifespan, so they become outdated or unusable after a specific period. This strategy encourages consumers to purchase new products more frequently, often leading to a cycle of waste and environmental harm. 

Understanding this concept is essential as it impacts consumer behavior, economic practices, and sustainability efforts in modern society. By being aware of programmed obsolescence, consumers can make more informed choices and support companies that prioritize sustainable practices.
